The best skincare routines for glowing, healthy skin this winter — dermatologists share their top tips

By Emily Thornton 11 June 2026, 09:30

A curated selection of winter skincare essentials recommended by Cambridge dermatologists. Photo: Unsplash

As temperatures drop and central heating kicks in across Cambridgeshire, skin experts are warning that now is the time to overhaul your daily skincare regime — before dryness, flaking and dullness take hold for the season.

We spoke to three leading dermatologists based in Cambridge to find out exactly which ingredients, products and habits are worth investing in this winter, and which popular trends are simply not worth the hype.

Start with a gentle, hydrating cleanser

"The single biggest mistake I see patients make in winter is continuing to use the same foaming cleanser they used in summer," says Dr Hannah Clarke, consultant dermatologist at Addenbrooke's Hospital. "Foaming cleansers can strip the skin's natural lipid barrier, and in cold weather that barrier is already under stress."

Instead, she recommends switching to a cream or oil-based cleanser for the colder months — something that removes make-up and daily grime without leaving skin feeling tight. Look for formulas containing glycerin, squalane or ceramides on the ingredient list.

Layer your moisturiser — correctly

The cardinal rule of winter skincare, according to Dr James Patel of the Cambridge Skin Clinic, is to apply moisturiser to slightly damp skin immediately after cleansing. "You're essentially locking in water molecules," he explains. "If you wait until your skin is completely dry, you've already lost a significant amount of hydration."

"Think of moisturiser as a seal, not a source. You need water in the skin before you apply it."
— Dr James Patel, Cambridge Skin Clinic

He recommends a two-step approach: a lightweight hyaluronic acid serum first, followed by a richer occlusive cream to seal everything in. For very dry skin types, a facial oil applied over the top can provide an additional protective layer overnight.

Don't skip SPF in winter

Perhaps the most common misconception in skincare is that sun protection becomes unnecessary once summer ends. UV radiation — specifically UVA rays — penetrates cloud cover and glass year-round, and is responsible for up to 80% of visible skin ageing, according to the British Association of Dermatologists.

"I tell every single patient the same thing: SPF every morning, 365 days a year, no exceptions," says Dr Sarah Okonkwo, who runs a private dermatology practice near Parker's Piece. "It is the single most evidence-backed anti-ageing intervention available — and it costs far less than any serum or device."

The ingredients worth spending on

With an overwhelming number of products lining pharmacy shelves, knowing which active ingredients are genuinely effective can feel impossible. Our dermatologists agreed on a shortlist of the most evidence-backed compounds:

Retinol — still the gold standard for stimulating cell turnover and reducing fine lines. Start with a low concentration (0.025–0.05%) two or three nights a week, and always apply SPF the following morning.

Niacinamide — a versatile vitamin B3 derivative that reduces redness, minimises pores and strengthens the skin barrier. Suitable for almost all skin types and compatible with most other actives.

Vitamin C — a powerful antioxidant that brightens uneven skin tone and offers some protection against UV-induced free radical damage. Apply in the morning, before SPF.

Whether you're building a routine from scratch or simply optimising what you already own, the consensus from Cambridge's skincare experts is the same: keep it consistent, keep it gentle, and always wear your SPF.
